Effective waste management is essential for maintaining environmental sustainability and public health. The "Smart Segregator " addresses the critical issue of unsegregated waste by automating the process of sorting dry and wet waste. This system utilizes advanced sensors, microcontrollers, and actuators to identify and segregate waste accurately, promoting efficient recycling and reducing human error. The design incorporates moisture and proximity sensors to classify waste and a servo motor-driven mechanism to direct it into designated compartments. The project demonstrates high accuracy and operational efficiency, with segregation achieved in under two seconds. The rapid urbanization and growing population have led to significant waste management challenges in cities. Improper waste segregation, inefficient collection, and lack of awareness contribute to environmental pollution and resource wastage. To address these issues, the integration of IoT-based smart systems in waste management has gained considerable attention. An IoT-based Smart Segregator is designed to automate the process of waste segregation and improve waste management efficiency, contributing to cleaner and more sustainable cities. Key Features of IoT-based Smart Segregator.


• Automatic Waste Segregation: The system uses moisture sensors to detect wet waste and direct it into a designated wet waste compartment, while dry waste (non-biodegradable) is directed to a separate dry waste compartment. This ensures that waste is properly segregated at the source, minimizing contamination and improving recycling and composting efforts.


• Real-time Data and Notifications: The IoT integration allows the system to collect and transmit data about the fill levels and waste types in real-time. This data is sent to a central waste management system, which can monitor the status of multiple dustbins across the city. Notifications are sent to municipal authorities when bins are near full capacity, ensuring timely collection and preventing overflow.


• Efficient Waste Collection and Route Optimization: The system can also provide route optimization for waste collection trucks. By tracking the fill levels of various dustbins, the system can suggest the most efficient routes for waste collection, reducing fuel consumption, traffic congestion, and air pollution associated with unnecessary collection trips


• Sustainability and Environmental Impact: By promoting proper waste segregation, the system reduces the amount of mixed waste sent to landfills, encouraging recycling and composting. This contributes to resource conservation, reduces landfill space requirements, and minimizes the environmental impact of waste disposal.

                    float getUltrasonicDistance(int trigPin, int echoPin) {
                      digitalWrite(trigPin, LOW);
                      delayMicroseconds(2);
                      digitalWrite(trigPin, HIGH);
                      delayMicroseconds(10);
                      digitalWrite(trigPin, LOW);
                      float duration = pulseIn(echoPin, HIGH);
                      float distance_cm = duration * 0.0343 / 2.0;
                      return distance_cm;
                    }
